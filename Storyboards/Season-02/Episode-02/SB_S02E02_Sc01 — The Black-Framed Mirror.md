- Source: The Story, Chapter 4 "Back Door Gospel" — the black-framed mirror + the watching shape
- Location: INT. VELVET ABYSS MIRROR ROOM — NIGHT
- Characters: Kane, (shape in mirror — do not name, do not show face)

The mirror is massive — floor to ceiling, black lacquered frame ringed with the same markings as the corridor but more concentrated, more formal. Kane steps in. He can see his own reflection. But something in the reflection isn't following his movements exactly. A shape — peripheral, patient, observer. This is the first Lich Lord presence on screen. Show nothing. REVEAL DISCIPLINE.

## Blocking & Camera
This scene requires the most careful production treatment in Season 2. The mirror should be a full-wall prop — in low-poly, this is an emissive plane with a second render camera placed slightly off-center in the scene to create the "wrong reflection" effect.

The shape in the mirror: it should be composed of the environmental darkness — not a ghostly figure, not a silhouette with identifiable features. The audience should not be sure if they saw it. Production notes in Low-Poly Asset Notes below.

Kane's movement is deliberate: he does not run. Running would suggest he knows what it is. He doesn't. He leaves because his instincts are functioning.

## Dialogue (adapted — consistent, not exact)

> KANE (V.O.): I found Evelyn in the corridor. I said: "We're leaving now."

## Lighting & Color
- E06: the mirror room has almost no lighting — the only ambient source is light leaking from the factory floor under the door, and the faint VFX05 emanation from the mirror frame itself
- The mirror is darkly reflective — it shows what's in front of it, but the ambient darkness behind that makes the "second render" shape possible
- VFX05 shimmer: a faint blue-gold pulse on the frame markings; not obvious — on the edge of visibility

## Audio / Bass Cue
- A01 (bass signature): in this room, the sustained pulse stops. Complete silence from A01 and A03. The room absorbs sound.
- A06 (cold/stillness): its closest relative — a long, low harmonic tone; almost inaudible; felt more than heard
- When Kane exits: A01 resumes at the same level it was before. The silence was the room. Not the building.

## Low-Poly Asset Notes
- E06 (mirror room) — production priority: the black mirror frame as full-wall plane; emissive P03 texture on frame
- P03 (black-framed mirror) — the mirror reflection itself: SECOND RENDER CAMERA positioned slightly off-Kane's-POV in the scene to create the shape in the reflection. The shape is the environmental darkness with a slightly different alpha — not a character mesh
- VFX05 (mirror shimmer) — emissive pulse on P03 frame texture; very subtle
- C01 Kane (stillness animation — barely breathing; controlled back-step)

## Continuity Flags
- **LICH LORD FIRST PRESENCE — REVEAL DISCIPLINE CRITICAL**: What appears in the mirror is never named, never described further. It is present, patient, watching. It behaves like something with full control of the situation. That is all. Do NOT add any narration explaining what it is.
- The silence of the room (A01 drops): this is a recurring signature — spaces where the Lich presence is concentrated absorb the ambient sound. When audiences have seen enough of these, the silence itself becomes a warning.
- Kane leaves. He does not tell Evelyn what he saw in the mirror. This is intentional — he will not discuss it until he can name it.
