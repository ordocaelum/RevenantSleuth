# SB_S02E01_Sc02 — The Stitched-Mouth Attacker
- Source: The Story, Chapter 4 "Back Door Gospel" — the stitched-mouth attacker fight
- Location: INT. VELVET ABYSS BASEMENT — NIGHT
- Characters: Kane, the Stitched-Mouth Attacker
- Beat summary: A figure drops from a maintenance gantry — wrong angles, wrong movement. The face: a mouth sewn shut with thick black thread. It moves like something operating a body from the inside: too-fluid pivots, joints bending at wrong degrees, velocity that doesn't match muscle mass. The fight is fast and brutal. Kane wins, but barely — the attacker doesn't respond to pain or shock the way people do.

## Blocking & Camera
The attacker's movement is the horror. The production must nail the puppet/wrong-angle quality: slow moments into sudden bursts; joints moving in non-anatomical directions at the moment of bass pulse; the total absence of pain response.

Fight is shot in close-medium — we stay with Kane's reactions as much as the violence itself. The attacker's wrongness should be communicated through economy of movement and the moments where physics doesn't add up.

The hold shot after the fight (shots 18–19): the attacker lying against the table, eyes open, not dead, not fleeing. Waiting. This should be deeply unsettling.

## Dialogue (adapted — consistent, not exact)
> KANE (V.O.): It dropped from the gantry. Landed wrong — like the knees had more options than knees should. I got a good look at the face.

> KANE (V.O.): The mouth was sewn shut. Not surgically. Not carefully. Thick black thread through the lips, multiple passes. Like someone had an opinion about what it should say and had taken steps.

> KANE (V.O.): I put a round into its shoulder. Center mass of the shoulder. Clean. It paused. Then it kept moving. Not through the pain — there was no pain. The pause was the problem. It paused and then it was still moving.

> KANE (V.O.): I won. I think. It hit the table and stopped moving and its eyes were open and calm and I backed away with my gun on it and I did not turn my back on it.
