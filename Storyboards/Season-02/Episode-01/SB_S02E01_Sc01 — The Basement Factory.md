# SB_S02E01_Sc01 — The Basement Factory
- Source: The Story, Chapter 4 "Back Door Gospel" — the basement "factory" + bodies + bound users + industrial fridge of vials
- Location: INT. VELVET ABYSS BASEMENT — NIGHT
- Characters: Kane
- Beat summary: Through the double doors and down, Kane finds what the Velvet Abyss really is: a processing facility. Metal tables with drains. Figures strapped to them. Industrial-gauge tubing running from the bound people's arms to collection containers. On the far wall, an industrial refrigeration unit — shelves upon shelves of vials. The cold is physical here — deeper than the December outside. This isn't just a drug lab. This is something that costs people something you can't name.

## Blocking & Camera
The basement is Kane's worst nightmare made concrete. The camera should keep him moving — he is not standing and gaping, he is processing and calculating. But we cut to hold shots of the bound figures and the fridge to let the horror settle.

The bound figures are alive. This is important. Their faces should be calm in a way that reads as deeply wrong — not beaten into submission, not drugged into unconsciousness. Compliant. Like they agreed to be here. (They didn't.)

Camera should stay slightly below eye level in this space — the low angle makes the metal tables more imposing, the fridge more monolithic.

## Dialogue (adapted — consistent, not exact)
> KANE (V.O.): The cold hit me at the bottom of the stairs. Not the December cold from outside. Something deliberate. Industrial. Like someone had specified a temperature.

> KANE (V.O.): Metal tables. Drains. Tubing. People strapped to both. Alive. Conscious. Calm. That last part was the worst part.

> KANE (V.O.): They weren't screaming. They weren't fighting the restraints. They were lying there like they'd chosen this. Like the music had asked them nicely.

> KANE (V.O.): The refrigeration unit was against the back wall. I opened the door. It exhaled cold like something had been kept at a very specific wrong temperature for a very long time.

> KANE (V.O.): Vials. Dozens. Maybe more. All labeled. Systematized. Whoever ran this operation had engineering habits. Had plans. Was counting supply against demand for something that wanted to remain whole.
