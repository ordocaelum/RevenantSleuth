## Blocking & Camera
This is the quietest scene in the season. No dialogue, minimal action. The entire scene is in Kane's face and hands.

The Polaroid reveal is staged in three beats: (1) face-down; (2) back of photo, the handwriting; (3) front of photo, the two men. Let each beat sit for 2–3 seconds.

Shot 13 — Kane after seeing the front of the photo — should hold for 4–5 full seconds. The audience needs to sit in this with him.

## Dialogue (adapted — consistent, not exact)
> KANE (V.O.): The Bridge file was gone. Whoever it was knew exactly which one they wanted. Surgical. The kind of removal that says this isn't random — this says I know your work and I know which thread to pull.

> KANE (V.O.): Then I found the photo. Face-down. Not there when the sergeant's team was through it — I would have seen it. It had been placed since.

> KANE (V.O.): "You still grab sleeves."

> KANE (V.O.): We were twenty-three in that photo. A training drill. I'd grabbed his sleeve to pull him clear of a demonstration exercise gone sideways. He'd thought it was funny for the next six months.

> KANE (V.O.): He thought a lot of things were funny that I didn't.
