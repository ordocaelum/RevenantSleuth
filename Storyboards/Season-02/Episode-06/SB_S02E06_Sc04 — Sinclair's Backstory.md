## Blocking & Camera
A moving dialogue scene — intimate, two characters in a box moving through the world. The information is delivered in Sinclair's characteristic economy: facts, then the implication she trusts Kane to draw.

Kane driving is important staging: he can't fully face her. He listens while watching the road. The not-facing lets Sinclair say this without it being a confrontation.

## Dialogue (adapted — consistent, not exact)
> SINCLAIR: The IA case. You should know the shape of it.

> KANE: You don't owe me that.

> SINCLAIR: I know. That's why I'm telling you. *(pause)* A detective. Eleven years, good record, liked by everyone who worked with him. I flagged him for a pattern of evidence handling inconsistencies. Small, consistent, across three cases.

> KANE: What happened?

> SINCLAIR: Pressure came from above his rank to close the inquiry. When the pushback comes from above the person you flagged, you know the flag was correct.

> KANE: What happened to the detective?

> SINCLAIR: Retired. Commendation. Clean record. I left the department eight months later before they found a reason to move me somewhere administrative.

> KANE: You think the Velvet Abyss has the same shape.

> SINCLAIR: I think institutional pressure that appears to protect a location rather than a person has the same function as pressure that protects a person. Something is being kept in operation.

> KANE: The deputy chief who called Grant.

> *(She looks at him.)*

> SINCLAIR: You noticed that too.

> KANE: Grant noticed it. She just couldn't say it the way she wanted to.
