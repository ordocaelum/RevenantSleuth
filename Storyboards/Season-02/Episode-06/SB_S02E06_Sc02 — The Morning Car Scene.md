## Blocking & Camera
The car is a contained space. The city moves outside; inside is still. This compression should reinforce the sense that the world outside is larger than what they can manage from inside a moving vehicle.

Sinclair's bass description (shots 9–11) is the scene's philosophical center. Hold on her face when she delivers it. This is her refining something she's been working out since the motel.

The Cho call ends the scene — and the weight of "something reviewed the room" lands in the silence after the call.

## Dialogue (adapted — consistent, not exact)
> SINCLAIR: It's past the elbow.

> KANE: I know.

> SINCLAIR: Cho should look at it again.

> KANE: That's where we're going.

> *(Silence. The city.)*

> SINCLAIR: I've been trying to find the right word for the bass thing.

> KANE: And?

> SINCLAIR: It's not sound. It's expectation. The interval before a note drops. Like my nervous system has memorized the gap and steps into it before the note arrives.

> KANE: That's a very specific description.

> SINCLAIR: I've had time to be specific.

> *(Phone buzzes. Cho.)*

> KANE: *(answers)* Cho.

> CHO: I left you a note at the front desk. Come get it.

> KANE: What's in it?

> CHO: The short version is: do not send anyone into that building alone. The longer version I will explain when you're in front of me and not on a phone.

> KANE: Something happened in the morgue last night.

> CHO: *(careful)* The vial is still locked. Something was in the room. I don't have a better description than that. Pick up the note.

> *(Call ends.)*

> *(Silence. The city outside the windshield.)*
