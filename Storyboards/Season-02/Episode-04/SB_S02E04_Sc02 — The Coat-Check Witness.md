## Blocking & Camera
Booth scene — standard two-shot interview format. The difference: the witness has to be sold on continuing each answer. She's not afraid of Kane. She's afraid of what she's describing.

Hold on her face when she describes the funeral smell — she's remembering it. Let the description arrive slowly.

## Dialogue (adapted — consistent, not exact)
> WITNESS: I worked coat check. Started in August, left in November.

> KANE: You leave voluntarily?

> WITNESS: There isn't a good word for it. I stopped going in.

> KANE: Why?

> WITNESS: The people. Most of them are just club people. Then some of them come back for their coats at close and they're... they come out quiet. Not tired-quiet. Switched-off quiet.

> KANE: They seemed intoxicated?

> WITNESS: Drunk people bounce off the walls. These ones walked in a straight line and they didn't look at you. Like walking was the whole job and everything else was optional.

> KANE: Did you ever see where they went after?

> WITNESS: The white van. Out back. I saw it three times from the alley. After close, they'd load the quiet ones in.

> KANE: Load them.

> WITNESS: That's the word. They walked but they needed — guidance. Direction. The two men by the door would point and they'd walk the direction they got pointed.

> KANE: You said three times. The last time?

> WITNESS: *(long beat)* The van smelled wrong. I was close to it. Just exhaust and winter and — *(stops)* It smelled like a viewing room. Like the chemical smell, but colder. With the engine running. That's not a smell that belongs on a running vehicle.

> KANE: So you stopped coming in.

> WITNESS: Would you have kept coming in?

> KANE: *(a beat)* No.
