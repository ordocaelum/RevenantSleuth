# SB_S02E05_Sc01 — Grant's Office
- Source: The Story, Chapter 5 "Cold Evidence" — Lt. Grant's office, institutional-pressure beat
- Location: INT. PRECINCT — LT. GRANT'S OFFICE — DAY
- Characters: Kane, Lt. Marisol Grant
- Beat summary: Grant calls Kane in. She has received pressure from above — the Velvet Abyss case is drawing attention in the wrong direction. She isn't corrupt; she is institutional. She is asking Kane to slow down in the language of procedure, which is the language of someone who knows you won't stop. She tells him what she can tell him. The rest is in what she doesn't say.

## Blocking & Camera
Grant's office is the precinct's controlled space. Clean sightlines, the desk as the divider. Neither character is comfortable in it right now — this meeting is happening at the edge of what they can say to each other.

Grant is not corrupt. She is worried. There is a difference, and the audience should feel it.

Kane standing throughout: he doesn't settle into this conversation because he can't afford to.

## Dialogue (adapted — consistent, not exact)
> GRANT: The Velvet Abyss complaint came through a deputy chief I've never spoken to.

> KANE: He ask you to close it?

> GRANT: He asked how it was progressing. That's different from closing it. That's a door left open.

> KANE: In case it needs to be closed from outside.

> GRANT: *(a beat)* What are you into?

> KANE: A club with a processing operation in the basement. A drug I can't name. People who respond to bass frequencies like they've been... re-tuned.

> GRANT: *(very still)* Re-tuned.

> KANE: That's the closest word I have.

> GRANT: Document it. Everything. The moment it hits your notebook, it exists independently of you.

> KANE: You think something's going to happen to me?

> GRANT: I think you keep going into buildings alone. Don't go into the Velvet Abyss again without a team.

> KANE: Understood.

> GRANT: *(reading him)* You're going back in.

> KANE: I said understood.

> GRANT: Those are two different things.

## Continuity Flags
- "Document everything. Every conversation, every evidence item." — Grant is giving Kane protection protocol. This implies she knows the channels are watching him.
- The deputy chief Kane has never met: institutional pressure from someone with ties to the Velvet Abyss's protection network. Not named. Not described as corrupt — but the call was not procedural.
- Grant's "don't go in again alone" — Kane's non-answer is characterization. He will go in again. He won't take a team.
