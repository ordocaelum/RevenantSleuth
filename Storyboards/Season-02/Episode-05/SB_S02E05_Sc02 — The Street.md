# SB_S02E05_Sc02 — The Street
- Source: The Story, Chapter 5 "Cold Evidence" — the street, the kneeling user struck by the car (command/bass beat), Sinclair's shoulder tense on bass hit
- Location: EXT. CITY STREET — DUSK / EARLY EVENING
- Characters: Kane, Evelyn Sinclair, Kneeling User (unnamed)
- Beat summary: Kane and Evelyn are walking back from the precinct. A figure kneels in the middle of the street — not in distress, just kneeling. Traffic swerves. Then the bass from a passing car hits — and the figure stands, walks, and the moment the beat drops, he steps directly in front of a vehicle. Not a jump. Not a fall. A step. The command was given. Evelyn's shoulder tenses the moment the bass hits — before Kane registers it.

## Blocking & Camera
The accident should happen fast and mostly off-camera — we hear it, we see Kane's reaction to it, we don't hold on it. The horror is not the accident. The horror is the sequence: bass hits → Evelyn's body responds → man stands → man steps → car.

The shot of Evelyn's shoulder tensing (shot 9) must come before the man stands (shot 10). That temporal priority is the key information of the scene. Hold on her shoulder in close-up for a single beat.

## Continuity Flags
- Evelyn's contamination threshold is escalating: motel → kitchen → street → ongoing. The phantom bass is becoming a warning organ for her. This is critical for her arc.
- K2 evidence: bass-command now confirmed as responsive to ANY bass source (not just the club). The club amplifies it; it's not exclusive to the club.
- "More than it was." — Evelyn does not say it's getting worse. She says it's more frequent. The distinction matters: she is tracking this clinically.
- REVEAL DISCIPLINE: no explanation of the mechanism. The audience and Kane are watching someone develop an involuntary response to the same thing that commands addicted users. The implications are clear and unspoken.
