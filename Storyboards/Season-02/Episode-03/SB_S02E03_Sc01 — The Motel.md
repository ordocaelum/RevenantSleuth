# SB_S02E03_Sc01 — The Motel
- Source: The Story, Chapter 5 "Cold Evidence" — motel, patching wounds, bite's "deep cold," Evelyn hears phantom bass
- Location: INT. MOTEL ROOM — NIGHT (late)
- Characters: Kane, Evelyn Sinclair
- Beat summary: A motel room — yellow lamp, industrial towels, two professionals patching damage. Kane's bite wound from the stitched-mouth attacker is doing something wrong. Not infected in any medical sense — cold. Spreading cold. The tissue around it isn't warm. Evelyn notices before Kane admits it. Then Evelyn hears the bass — there is no bass. They are miles from the club. The phantom sound has followed her. She doesn't say "phantom." She says: "I can still hear it."

## Blocking & Camera
This scene is intimate and understated. The violence is over; this is the processing of evidence in the body.

The bathroom doorway framing is key: both of them occupying the liminal space between the utilitarian sink and the motel room. Not fully in either space.

Hold on the moment when Evelyn says "I can still hear it" — hold on her face. Hold on Kane's. Don't cut away too fast.

## Dialogue (adapted — consistent, not exact)
> KANE (V.O.): The motel was seventeen dollars above what I'd normally tolerate for a safe place to regroup. I was paying the extra seventeen.

> EVELYN: Let me see the bite.

> KANE: It's fine.

> EVELYN: Show me the bite.

> *(She touches the margin. Her fingers retract.)*

> EVELYN: How long has it been cold like this?

> KANE: Since it happened.

> EVELYN: It's spreading.

> KANE: I can tell.

> *(She wraps it anyway. He doesn't stop her.)*

> KANE: It's not infected.

> EVELYN: I know. *(a beat)* That's not the word for it.

> *(Silence.)*

> EVELYN: Do you hear the bass?

> KANE: From the club? We're fifteen miles —

> EVELYN: I know how far we are. *(quietly)* It's not... it's not a sound exactly. It's more like... expectation. Like the interval before a note drops.

> KANE: You're tired.

> EVELYN: *(steady)* I can still hear it.
