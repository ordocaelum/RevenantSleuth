## Blocking & Camera
The apartment should feel smaller in daylight — the familiar space made foreign by the police presence. Kane moves through his own home like a suspect in it.

The case box beat should be played quietly — Kane recognizes the disturbance, doesn't broadcast it. The sergeant is watching him. Kane knows the sergeant is watching him.

## Dialogue (adapted — consistent, not exact)
> KANE (V.O.): They'd processed my apartment with the professional care of people who had already decided what happened. Crime scene courtesy. I'd given them the version of events that was accurate where accuracy didn't cost me anything.

> SERGEANT: We need your clothes, Detective. The ones you wore last night.

> KANE: Not yet.

> SERGEANT: It's standard procedure —

> KANE: I know what it is. When I'm done with the active investigation, you'll have them. Not before.

> *(The sergeant writes. Kane moves to the case box.)*

> KANE (V.O.): They'd been through it. I could tell by the order of the files — I stack by date, not by subject. Someone had restacked by subject and thought I wouldn't notice.

> KANE: Who else has been in this apartment?

> SERGEANT: Crime scene team. Properly authorized.

> KANE: The case box wasn't part of the evidence inventory.

> SERGEANT: *(a beat)* I'll look into that.

> KANE (V.O.): He wouldn't look into it. The case box was the wrong kind of concern for the wrong kind of police work. Someone had been here before the crime scene team.
