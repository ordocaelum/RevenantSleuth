## Blocking & Camera
This entire scene is contained within the monitor frame — it is footage, not direct narrative. The inner shots are all POV from the rookie's perspective, slightly below his eye-line (chest-mounted camera).

Key rhythm: the footage should breathe with false calm before the car door opens. Two long static shots (the road; the car) before anything happens. Then — when it does — it is fast, unclear, and abrupt. No slow reveals of the attacker. One clean motion. Cut.

The cutaway from the footage back to the bullpen should feel like a slap — not edited dramatically but cut sharply, mid-motion, back to the dead-quiet room.

## Lighting & Color
- All footage shots: body-cam night-exposure filter — washed-out, grey-blue-green tint, heavy vignette, scan-line artifact
- The blood reads as black-brown on the footage (not red) — the footage's color response makes it worse
- When the camera rolls in the dirt: the sky is grey, the road is grey, the smear is slightly darker grey. Nothing vivid. Everything drained.

## Audio / Bass Cue
- A06 silence builds slowly — ambient sound (wind, road, crickets) fades as the rookie approaches the car
- The car shifting: a metallic clunk, realistic and wrong
- The blood pouring: a heavy liquid sound — not dramatic, just physical and wet
- The scream: cut off like a cord snapping (prose description — mid-note, gone)
- After: silence. Two beats. Then the bullpen ambient returns (someone shifts in their chair; a pen drops)

## Low-Poly Asset Notes

- The sedan windows: UV-mapped dark-red smear texture — no complex fluid simulation

- The blood: dark particle burst + flat-plane splatter decal on ground. Not anatomically detailed.
