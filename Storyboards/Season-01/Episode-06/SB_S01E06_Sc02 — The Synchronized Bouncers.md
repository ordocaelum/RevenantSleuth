# SB_S01E06_Sc02 — The Synchronized Bouncers
- Source: The Story, Chapter 4 "Back Door Gospel" — taking down the two bouncers; the synchronized obedience tell
- Location: EXT. VELVET ABYSS REAR ALLEY — NIGHT
- Characters: Kane, Bouncer 1 (throat), Bouncer 2 (knee)
- Beat summary: Kane takes down both bouncers — but before he acts, he notices something wrong: they shift at the exact same moment, like something tugged their shoulders. The bass swell. Perfect unison. Not a coincidence. After the fight, the bass swells again — and both injured bouncers go slack, obedient, silent. Like they were told to stop. This is K2's first direct visual proof.

## Blocking & Camera
The fight is fast — 4-6 seconds of action. No slow motion, no spectacle. The violence is working-class.

The key beats to hold: (1) the pre-action unison movement of the bouncers — hold on them for 2 full beats with the camera static; (2) the post-action bass swell silencing both injured men — hold for 3 beats; let the sudden silence from the screaming man be genuinely shocking.

Shot 17 and 18: cut between the two men rapidly — both showing the same instant transition from pain/panic to vacant compliance. The rhythm of the cuts should mirror the bass beat.
