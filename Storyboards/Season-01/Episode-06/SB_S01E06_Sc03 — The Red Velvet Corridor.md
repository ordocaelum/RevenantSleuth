## Blocking & Camera
The corridor is the episode's visual centerpiece — the production should invest here. The narrow geometry, the warm amber pools of light from the sconces, the deep shadow between them.

Kane moves through carefully — not quickly. He is reading the environment. The markings are on every wall panel; the camera should hold on several of them without commentary or dramatic music.

The double doors at the end of the corridor should feel like an architectural inevitability — everything in this space has been pointing at them.

## Dialogue (adapted — consistent, not exact)
> KANE (V.O.): Red velvet. Gold trim. The kind of hallway a man builds when he wants you to understand you're moving through someone's property.

> KANE (V.O.): The markings were here too. Evelyn had called them archaic. Up close, that wasn't the right word. Archaic implied old-fashioned. These felt... current. Like they'd been put here recently, with full intention.

> KANE (V.O.): At the end of the corridor: double doors. Heavy. Gold engraving. The kind of engraving you find in buildings built by people who didn't need permission.

> KANE (V.O.): I pushed through.

## Lighting & Color
- E04: deep red walls; gold trim emissive (warm gold point light from sconces); full darkness between each sconce
- The corridor has no relief light — you are either in an amber pool or in shadow
- The double doors at the end: lit from the sconce nearest to them; the gold engraving catches the amber light and makes the markings look almost decorative until you're close
