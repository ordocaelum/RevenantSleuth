## Blocking & Camera
The key blocking split: Evelyn enters the club (camera stays with her until she's absorbed by the crowd — then cuts to Kane moving). This establishes the physical separation that will drive the comms tension.

From the rooftop, Kane's view of the club is the audience's view — wide, elevated, slightly powerless. He has good sightlines and zero direct access. This is deliberate staging for the vulnerability that follows.

The upper window glowing faintly behind curtains: hold on it for two beats after Kane establishes his rooftop position. One window is lit. Someone is up there.

## Continuity Flags
- The one lit upper window: significant. Someone in that office is watching the entrance. This will be the Voss reveal (he's in that office when he seizes the comms channel in Sc01 of S01E05).
- The bass through brick: Kane feels it on the rooftop. This is not yet the obedience reflex — it's just overwhelming physical presence. The character bible's "a heartbeat too loud to be natural" is the right framing.
- The fire escape "rusted and bent like it had been through a war": a detail from the prose. Keep it — cheap to model and texturally important as a signal that Kane is improvising with what's available, not executing a polished operation.
