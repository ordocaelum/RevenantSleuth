## Blocking & Camera
The key staging choice: the figure carried out of the back door should be visible enough to read as human and carried, but not clear enough to confirm it's Evelyn. Distance and angle do the work. This uncertainty is the engine.

Kane's movement to the fire escape is not frantic. He moves the way people move when they've already decided and the decision is costing them something. Not a sprint — a determined walk that is almost a sprint.

## Dialogue (adapted — consistent, not exact)
> KANE (V.O.): The comms had been dead for six minutes. In six minutes, a lot of things can go wrong. In six minutes, someone can be moved.

> KANE (V.O.): I saw them bring someone out the back. Two men. A third figure between them — not walking on their own.

> KANE (V.O.): I had two options. I could stay on the roof and maintain position. Or I could go in.

> KANE (V.O.): I'd been choosing option two for twenty years. Why change what works.

> KANE (V.O.): *(at the fire escape top — looking back once)* I wasn't going to get her out by watching from a safe distance.

## Continuity Flags
- The carried figure may not be Evelyn — the prose notes it was "a body carried out" which Kane interprets as reason to go in. The adaptation preserves this ambiguity.
- "Why change what works" — this line reflects Kane's "option 2 for 20 years" character pattern. It's also slightly self-aware: he knows this is probably going to cost him something. He goes anyway. This is the "Ch.1–2 impulsive ethical line" per Character Bible.
- The fire escape is the same one he used to get UP the building — he goes back down the same way. The route is already known: competence under pressure.
