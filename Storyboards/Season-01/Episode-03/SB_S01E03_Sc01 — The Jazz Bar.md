## Blocking & Camera
The bar is narrow — the two characters are close by geometry. But there is careful emotional distance: they sit side by side, not face to face. Most of this scene is about the bar mirror — Kane watches her reflection first, then she turns. The mirror behind the bar is significant as a device (unlike the Velvet Abyss mirror, this one is honest — it shows what's there).

Evelyn's glass-lift (acknowledging Kane's entry before he reaches her) should be a very small motion — barely perceptible. It is, however, timed precisely to the moment Kane crosses the threshold. She knew.

## Dialogue (adapted — consistent, not exact)
> KANE: *(sitting)* Evelyn.

> EVELYN: *(not turning yet)* Detective Kane.

> *(A beat. The bartender slides a drink toward Kane.)*

> EVELYN: *(still forward)* You're back in the dirt.

> KANE: I never left. Just different streets.

> *(She turns. First direct look.)*

> EVELYN: Talk.

## Lighting & Color
- E11 interior: deep amber throughout — this is the warmest scene in the episode, which is deliberate. Kane is meeting an ally. The warmth will not last.
- The bar mirror: undecorated, honest. The reflections are accurate — no supernatural delay, no wrong color. Contrast with the Velvet Abyss mirror that will appear in S02E02.
- Evelyn lit from the right (bar amber backlight) — warm. She looks human and present.
- Kane lit slightly cooler from the ambient city-glow bleeding through the bar's small front window — the city's blue-grey following him in

- First Evelyn appearance on-screen — establish the burgundy coat, the composed posture, the hold-eye-contact-too-long tell
