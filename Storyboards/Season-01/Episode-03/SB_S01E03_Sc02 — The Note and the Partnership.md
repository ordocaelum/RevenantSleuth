# SB_S01E03_Sc02 — The Note and the Partnership
- Source: The Story, Chapter 2 "Alliance Forged in Darkness" — recruiting Evelyn; showing the note
- Location: INT. JAZZ BAR — NIGHT (continuous from Sc01)
- Characters: Kane, Evelyn
- Beat summary: Kane lays everything out — the body-cam, the family, the drug, his apartment. Evelyn files it without flinching. He slides the note across. She reads it without touching it. "You think Max is involved?" They agree to move on the club — not from trust, but from mutual utility. The partnership is formed in the same breath as the acknowledgment that trust makes people sloppy.

## Blocking & Camera
The blocking here is entirely at the bar — side-by-side. When Kane tells her about the apartment, neither character moves dramatically. She is the audience for an efficient briefing, not a confession.

The note is the scene's pivot point. When it slides across the bar, the camera holds on it — not on Kane or Evelyn — for two beats. Then cuts to her reading it. Then to her asking the Max question.

When they stand to leave, they do not leave together. Evelyn goes first; Kane watches her go; then he follows a beat later. Two strangers, by design.

## Dialogue (adapted — consistent, not exact)
> KANE: Body-cam footage. A family. Five bodies rearranged in a car. The drug in the father's blood is new — layered. Something the lab can't fully classify. The first confirmed suspect took four rounds center mass and kept walking. Then a woman at my apartment...

> *(He sets the note on the bar. She looks.)*

> EVELYN: You think Max is involved?

> KANE: Max is the kind of man who leaves messages with bodies. And he knows I read them.

> *(A beat.)*

> EVELYN: If we do this — we do it clean.

> KANE: Clean isn't on the menu.

> EVELYN: It is if you want to survive.

> KANE: I'm not asking because I trust you.

> EVELYN: Good. Trust makes people sloppy.

> *(She looks at the note one more time.)*

> EVELYN: I'll need to see the building. The layout.

> KANE: Tomorrow.

> *(She stands.)*

> EVELYN: We leave separately.

> KANE: Two strangers. Yeah.
