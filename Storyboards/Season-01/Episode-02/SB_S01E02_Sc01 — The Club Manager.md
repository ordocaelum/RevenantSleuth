# SB_S01E02_Sc01 — The Club Manager
- Source: The Story, Chapter 1 "Crimson Veil" — arrival at The Velvet Abyss; confronting Tony DeMarco
- Location: INT. THE VELVET ABYSS MAIN FLOOR — NIGHT
- Characters: Kane, Tony DeMarco, two bouncers (background)
- Beat summary: Kane walks into the club, finds DeMarco behind the bar, and makes the moment honest — with his fist. What he gets in exchange is partial truth: wealthy distributors, no names, instructions, and something upstairs that makes people "come back different." Kane walks out with a direction and a mistake he doesn't know he made yet.

## Blocking & Camera
The bar is the arena. Kane approaches DeMarco from the entrance side — the bouncers flank at the bar's edges. DeMarco is behind the bar, which gives him an illusion of a boundary. Kane doesn't respect the boundary.

When Kane hits DeMarco, there is no dramatic wind-up shot. It is cut from DeMarco's expression to the impact. No slow motion. The violence is punctuation, not spectacle.

After the information is extracted, Kane does not look back at DeMarco. He walks back through the crowd as if the conversation is already filed and moved past.

## Dialogue (adapted — consistent, not exact)
> DE MARCO: Detective Kane. We're private tonight.

> KANE: You don't look private. You look scared.

> DE MARCO: I don't know what you—

> KANE: I don't think. I watch. I listen. I bury what I can't fix.

> *(The punch. DeMarco folds.)*

> KANE: *(close, low)* You don't get to play dumb. Not with what I saw tonight.

> DE MARCO: *(rasping)* Okay. Okay. Just—

> KANE: The drug. What's it called?

> DE MARCO: I don't know the name. It came through people I didn't know. Wealthy. Quiet. Not street level.

> KANE: Who do they answer to?

> DE MARCO: They don't give names. They give instructions.

> KANE: Where does it come from?

> DE MARCO: *(tightening)* Upstairs. Back rooms. People go up. And they come back different. Like the lights are on but… nobody's steering.

> *(Beat.)*

> DE MARCO: If you're smart, Kane — you walk out. Forget this address.

> KANE: *(turning away)* Then it's lucky I'm not smart like that.

## Lighting & Color
- Main floor: purple overhead wash + strobe pulse (VFX02 screen-pulse on each bass beat — very subtle)
- At the bar: amber practical backlight from bottles — this makes DeMarco look warm, almost normal, until he doesn't
- Kane's face: lit from below by the bar backlight — slightly menacing from below; purple from above
- The bouncers: silhouetted against the dance floor — they are shapes, not characters yet

## Audio / Bass Cue
- A03 (diegetic club music): full volume as Kane enters; the bass thumps "thick enough to rattle teeth" (prose)
- VFX02 (bass screen pulse): synced to the diegetic bass — the frame darkens slightly on each pulse; Kane's entry into the club should be accompanied by this effect for the first time
- A07 (score): under the club music, the noir jazz element surfaces briefly during the bar conversation — then club music takes back over
- When Kane walks out: A03 cuts abruptly (door closing), and the city quiet replaces it — the contrast of inside/outside sound design

## Continuity Flags
- K1 flag confirmed: The Velvet Abyss is the distribution point; DeMarco confirms upstairs activity
- "Lights are on but nobody's steering" — this is the clearest early K0/K1 description of what the drug does; it will echo through the series
- The bouncers standing too still with hands near waistbands: note their posture. In S01E06, the significance of bouncers moving in unison will land harder if this earlier pair was subtly odd.
- Kane walking away from a man he just hit, without looking back: establish the character rhythm now
- DeMarco is alive when Kane leaves. The head in the apartment (Sc03 of this episode) is the consequence.
